H O N G K O N G


A BRIEF GUIDE TO THE BUSTLING CITY OF LIGHT


food | shopping | sightseeing | map


F O O D

Egglets


a local Hong Kong food resembling inverted, rounded waffles. Featured in this restaurant as a component of a sundae.

Egg Tarts


tarts with an egg custard filling that can be found in Hong Kong and other Asian countries. Crumbles deliciously with light, gelatinous consistency.

Dim Sum


a Cantonese cuisine that involves multiple small dishes like pork buns and xiumai. Featured in this restaurant as cute, cartoonish versions.

S H O P P I N G

Temple Street


a night market with many local snacks & trinkets. Prepare to haggle for your life on one of the busiest streets in Hong Kong. Open from 6 pm to 11 pm every night.

Mong Kok


a district chock full of shops: brand stores, local boutiques and street stalls. Wander the bustling streets in search of snacks and souvenirs, or just to window-shop!

Times Square


a grand shopping centre boasting 230+ stores located in Causeway Bay. Shop from its high-end stores and continue your shopping trip in nearby malls or streets as well!

S I G H T S E E I N G

Ocean Park


This underwater-themed park includes aquariums, pandas, roller coasters and more! Prepare yourself for a whole day of fun in this theme park.

Goldfish Street


Located in Mong Kok, this street features a collection of goldfish stores. It becomes especially aesthetically pleasing at night as neon lights shine.

Victoria Harbour


Stroll along the harbour and enjoy the iconic Hong Kong skyline. Don't miss the light show at 8 pm every night!
